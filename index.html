About

Research at the intersection of perception, interaction, and security.

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at the University of Wyoming, advised by Dr. Diksha Shukla. My research builds practical ML systems for immersive environments: object-aware Gaussian Splatting for interaction, AR display side-channel analysis, and active authentication from gaze, head, hand, and EEG signals.

I enjoy turning research ideas into complete systems — collecting data, designing models, building pipelines, evaluating across realistic conditions, and integrating results into XR platforms such as Unity, OpenXR, HoloLens, and Quest.

RigidGS
3D Vision · Neural Rendering

RigidGS — Object-decoupled Gaussian Splatting for VR interaction

A physics-aware 3D Gaussian Splatting pipeline that decouples objects from scenes so users can interact with Gaussian-rendered environments in VR. The system combines COLMAP, SAM2 mask propagation, LaMa inpainting, boundary regularization, and Unity/OpenXR runtime integration.

3D Gaussian SplattingPyTorchSAM2Unity/OpenXRRigid Body Interaction
LightLeaks Pipeline
Computer Vision · Applied AI Security

LightLeaks — Sensitive information exposure through AR display leakage

A computer-vision and LLM-assisted pipeline for reconstructing sensitive content from light passively leaked by optical see-through MR displays. The pipeline includes ROI extraction, temporal fusion, OCR enhancement, and semantic reconstruction.

OpenCVOCRMediaPipeLLM reasoning
Domain informed feature extractors and lightweight ResNet based models
Behavioral Biometrics · XR Authentication

Active authentication for mixed reality from gaze, head, and hand signals

A multimodal authentication research line using natural in-headset behavior: eye movement dynamics, 6-DoF head motion, hand-controller kinematics, and EEG. The emphasis is practical evaluation, cross-session robustness, and longitudinal stability.
